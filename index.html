Types of Lumpia in the Philippines



Tutorial: How to make Lumpiang Shanghai

Lumpiang Shanghai

Lumpiang Shanghai is a popular Filipino dish often served at parties and special occasions.
It is a type of spring roll filled with a mixture of ground pork, vegetables (such as carrots and onions),
and seasonings. The filling is wrapped in a thin lumpia wrapper and then deep-fried until golden and crispy.
Lumpiang Shanghai is typically served with a dipping sauce, often sweet and sour or vinegar-based.
It's a well-loved dish in the Philippines due to its flavorful filling and crunchy texture, making it a staple in Filipino cuisine.


Tutorial: How to make Lumpiang Togue

Lumpiang Togue

Lumpiang Togue is vegetable version of the lumpiang shanghai, this would be the version that uses togue or bean sprouts instead of meat.
This is the most drastic tweak to the recipe, and its an easy and filling swap. The bean sprouts are the main ingredient, and its accompanied
by slivers of carrots, cabbage, onions, and sometimes, a little bit of ground pork or chicken to give it that meaty taste that you might miss in your lumpia.


Tutorial: How to make Lumpiang Gulay

Lumpiang Gulay

Lumpiang Gulay is a Filipino vegetable spring roll that is popular for its light and healthy ingredients. "Gulay" means vegetables in Filipino, and this lumpia variation
is filled primarily with a mix of vegetables like cabbage, carrots, green beans, and sometimes sweet potatoes or bean sprouts. It is usually served with a dipping sauce,
such as vinegar with garlic and chili or a sweet and sour sauce. Lumpiang Gulay is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as an appetizer, snack, or even a light meal.


Tutorial: How to make Lumpiang Turon

Lumpiang Turon

Turon is a popular Filipino snack and dessert made from ripe saba bananas (a variety of banana found in the Philippines) and sometimes includes slices of jackfruit.
The banana and jackfruit are wrapped in a lumpia wrapper, similar to a spring roll, and then deep-fried.Turon is a beloved street food in the Philippines and is often
enjoyed as a sweet treat or merienda (afternoon snack). It's simple to make, yet its combination of textures and flavors—crisp wrapper, tender banana, and the sweetness
of caramelized sugar—makes it a delightful and satisfying snack.


Tutorial: How to make Fresh Lumpia

Fresh Lumpia

Fresh Lumpia, known as "Lumpiang Sariwa" in Filipino, is a type of spring roll that is served fresh and not fried, unlike other lumpia varieties. This dish features a soft,
crepe-like wrapper made from flour, eggs, and water, which encases a filling of fresh, sautéed vegetables. Fresh Lumpia is a light, healthy dish that offers a refreshing contrast
to the deep-fried versions of lumpia. It is typically served as an appetizer or a light meal and is popular at gatherings and special occasions in the Philippines.
