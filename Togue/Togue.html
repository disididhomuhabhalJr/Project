How to make Lumpiang Togue

ingredients:

  • 2 cups togue (bean sprouts)
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup green beans, julienned
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1/4 cup celery, chopped (optional)
  • 1/4 cup tofu, diced (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lumpia wrappers
  • Cooking oil for sautéing and frying

Dipping Sauce (Optional):

  • Vinegar
  • Soy sauce
  • Minced garlic
  • Chili (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables:
    • Wash and prepare all the vegetables. Julienne the carrot and green beans, shred the cabbage, and chop the onion, garlic, and celery.
  2. Cook the Filling:
    • Heat a tablespoon of cooking oil in a pan over medium heat.
    • Sauté the garlic and onion until they become translucent and aromatic.
    • Add the tofu (if using) and cook until slightly golden.
    • Add the carrots and green beans, and sauté for about 2-3 minutes.
    • Add the cabbage, celery, and togue (bean sprouts). Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
    • Season with soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), salt, and pepper. Stir everything well.
    • Remove from heat and let the filling cool slightly.
  3. Wrap the Lumpia:
    • Place a lumpia wrapper on a flat surface with one corner pointing towards you, forming a diamond shape.
    • Spoon about 1-2 tablespoons of the filling onto the center of the wrapper.
    • Fold the bottom corner over the filling, tuck in the sides, and roll it up tightly. Dab a little water or beaten egg on the top corner to seal the wrapper.
  4. Fry the Lumpia:
    • Heat enough cooking oil in a pan or deep fryer over medium heat.
    • Once the oil is hot, carefully add the lumpia in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
    • Fry until the lumpia is golden brown and crispy, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking.
    • Remove the lumpia from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve the Lumpia Togue hot with your preferred dipping sauce. A simple vinegar with minced garlic and a dash of soy sauce works well.

Enjoy your delicious, homemade Lumpiang Togue!
